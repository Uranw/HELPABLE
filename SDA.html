What is SDA

Specialist Disability Accommodation (SDA) refers to housing specifically designed to support people with significant disabilities who require more specialised housing solutions.

The accommodation is tailored to meet the needs of individuals with complex support requirements, such as those needing specific equipment, home modifications, and/or access to 24/7 support staff. SDA homes are built or modified to incorporate features that assist with mobility, communication, and daily living activities, giving you independence, safety, and quality of life in your home.

How to access SDA funding

Accessing SDA is subject to eligibility criteria and availability of NDIS funding. Interchange can help you by working closely with NDIS planners, Support Coordinators, and housing providers to help navigate the process effectively and help with alternative solutions should you not be eligible.

Zero Tolerance Against Abuse

People with disability, like everyone else, have the right to live free of violence, abuse, neglect, and exploitation. Zero Tolerance is an initiative led by National Disability Services to assist disability service providers to understand and improve practices and keep you safe.

At Interchange, we follow a Zero Tolerance approach. It is a personal and organisational commitment to always have our eyes open and always do better to protect the rights of the people we support. We encourage everyone who wants to raise concerns to please do so. Our Whistleblower policy will protect you from intimidation, disadvantage or reprisal, your identity will be protected and you can choose to be anonymous.

We encourage our employees, people with disability, families, supporters and the general public, to expose wrong doings and alert the authorities if you have any concerns.
